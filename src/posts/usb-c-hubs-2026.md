A laptop with two USB-C ports and nothing else is a productivity bottleneck. A bad USB-C hub makes it worse — dropped connections, slow charging pass-through, or a hub that runs hot enough to throttle everything connected to it.

After testing eight hubs over six weeks with a MacBook Pro and a Windows laptop, here's what actually works.

## What to Look for in a USB-C Hub

**Port selection.** Most people need: HDMI (4K@60Hz minimum), two USB-A ports, one USB-C data port, SD card reader, and 100W power delivery pass-through. Anything less is a compromise.

**Chipset quality.** Cheap hubs use low-quality chipsets that cause dropped connections, flickering displays, or heat buildup that throttles performance. This isn't something you can see in product photos.

**Build temperature.** I measured each hub's surface temperature during a 2-hour work session. Anything above 45°C is a concern for longevity.

## The Tested Hubs

| Hub | Ports | PD | 4K@60Hz | Avg Temp |
| --- | ----- | -- | ------- | -------- |
| Anker 555 (7-in-1) | 7 | 100W | Yes | 38°C |
| Anker 332 (5-in-1) | 5 | 85W | Yes | 36°C |
| CalDigit TS4 | 18 | 98W | Yes | 41°C |
| Satechi Slim (5-in-1) | 5 | 60W | No (30Hz) | 33°C |
| Amazon Basics 7-in-1 | 7 | 60W | No (30Hz) | 44°C |

## The Winner: Anker 555 (7-in-1)

The Anker 555 consistently delivered 4K@60Hz with no dropped connections across both laptops. Power delivery held at 100W even under load. Surface temperature stayed well under 40°C during extended sessions.

At ~$50 it's not the cheapest option, but it's the one I'd recommend without hesitation for most home office setups.

## Best Budget Pick: Anker 332 (5-in-1)

If you only need one display output and can live without an SD card reader, the 332 does everything right at ~$30. Same reliable chipset as the 555, just fewer ports.

## Avoid

The Amazon Basics 7-in-1 ran hot (44°C average) and caused display flicker on the Windows laptop during video calls. The Satechi Slim's 30Hz display output is a dealbreaker for anything beyond a second document reference screen.

## The Clean Desk Payoff

A single good USB-C hub eliminates four to six separate cables from your desk. Combined with a monitor arm and a cable management tray, it's one of the highest-impact purchases for desk aesthetics relative to cost.

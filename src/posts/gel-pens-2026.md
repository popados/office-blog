Finding a great gel pen sounds easy — there are hundreds of options. After buying and writing with over 40 different pens across three months of daily note-taking, planning, and journaling, a few clear winners emerged.

## What We Tested For

- Ink flow and consistency (no skipping or blobbing)
- Comfort over long writing sessions
- Dry time and smear resistance
- Refill availability
- Value per pen

## The Top Picks

### 1. Pilot G2 Fine (0.7mm)

The G2 is the benchmark. Smooth, consistent, and available everywhere. The 0.7mm tip hits the sweet spot between precision and flow. Works reliably on cheap copy paper *and* premium dot-grid notebooks without skipping.

**Best for:** Everyday office use, note-taking  
**Price:** ~$14 for a 12-pack

### 2. Zebra Sarasa Clip (0.5mm)

A serious contender that outperforms the G2 in one area: dry time. The Sarasa's ink dries noticeably faster, which matters for left-handed writers or anyone who smears wet ink. The retractable clip mechanism is also one of the best in this price range.

**Best for:** Journaling, left-handed writers  
**Price:** ~$19 for a 10-pack

### 3. Uni-ball Signo 207 (0.7mm)

If forgery-resistant ink matters to you (and for check-writing it should), the Signo 207 is built with UNI Super Ink that bonds to paper fibers. It also writes smoothly enough to use daily without feeling like a security-first compromise.

**Best for:** Financial documents, all-purpose use  
**Price:** ~$10 for an 8-pack

### 4. Pentel EnerGel RTX (0.7mm)

The EnerGel dries faster than almost any other gel pen in this roundup. Its liquid-gel hybrid ink feels closer to a rollerball — very fast, very smooth. Slightly wetter than the G2, so paper quality matters more.

**Best for:** Fast writers, anyone who hates smearing  
**Price:** ~$12 for a 5-pack

## The Verdict

For most people, **Pilot G2** remains the default choice — widely available, consistent, and well-priced. If you write fast and smear easily, swap to the **Pentel EnerGel** or **Zebra Sarasa**. And if you value fast-drying ink above all else, the Sarasa Clip wins.

All four earned a spot in my daily rotation, and none of them have disappointed after months of use.

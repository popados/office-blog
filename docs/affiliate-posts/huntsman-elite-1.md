# Razer Huntsman Elite Review: Speed-Running Your Way to a Better Setup

*Clicky. Glowy. Expensive. But hear me out.*

---

Let's be real — your keyboard is the one peripheral you're touching every single second of every session. Miss a skill rotation because your switches lagged? That's a wipe. Fumble a keybind in the final circle? That's a loss. The Razer Huntsman Elite is here to make sure the fault line runs somewhere else — preferably at your teammates.

**Verdict:** A hall-of-fame flex for your battlestation, if your wallet can take the aggro.

---

## The Price — Boss-Level Entry Fee

The Huntsman Elite launched at a wallet-shredding $199, which in keyboard terms puts you firmly in "I need to justify this to my significant other" territory. Is it worth it? Compared to budget boards sitting at $40–$60, you're paying for genuine engineering, not just RGB snake oil. Think of it as the difference between a legendary drop and a white-quality loot. You *can* clear the dungeon with a white drop. But why would you?

**Affiliate tip:** Prices have softened since launch — [grab it on Amazon](https://www.amazon.com) or check Best Buy for bundles and open-box deals that bring it closer to the $120–$150 range. Your bank account will thank you.

---

## The Switches — Actuating at the Speed of Plot Armor

This is where the Huntsman Elite goes full tryhard, and honestly, it earns it.

Razer's optical switches use a light sensor inside the switch — when you press a key, a receiver is activated by a light signal, instantly actuating the command. It's the only clicky switch to actuate at a 1.5mm actuation distance, 30% shorter than other clicky mechanical switches, at just 45g of actuation force. Reset and actuation happen at the exact same point, maximizing APM during fast-paced games.

Translation for the non-engineers: these things are *fast*. Like, "I swear I didn't press that" fast. TechRadar noted they had literally never typed quicker on another keyboard — and that's not sponsored hyperbole, that's hands-on benchmark talk. For FPS players, MOBA mains, or anyone spamming abilities on cooldown, the actuation speed is a genuine competitive edge, not a marketing gimmick.

The purple optical switches also have a satisfying *click* on the way down — tactile feedback that tells your brain "confirmed, the spell is cast, enemies are crying." No mushy membrane nonsense here.

---

## The Sound — Clicky But Not Chaotic

Let's address the elephant in the Discord call: yes, it's a clicky keyboard. Your squadmates *will* hear it. Your parents *will* ask what that noise is at 2am.

That said, the click is crisp and defined rather than rattly — a key stabilizer bar on the top of the switch ensures keycap motion stays level and consistent no matter which corner of the keycap you press, which eliminates that hollow, wobbly wobble that plagues cheaper boards. The overall sound profile is satisfying without being a jackhammer. If you're on a headset during ranked, your teammates are safe. Mostly.

If you stream or sit in open lobbies, consider dampening rings — a $10 add-on that turns "clicky chaos" into "satisfying ASMR."

---

## The Backlight — Chroma Did Not Come To Play

Okay. You know how some keyboards say "RGB" and mean "one shade of red cycling through three modes"? The Huntsman Elite is not that keyboard.

Full 4-side underglow with 38 customization zones means this board doesn't just light up — it *performs*. The wrist rest joins the party too, featuring Chroma-enabled underglow lighting across 20 additional customization zones. Razer Chroma syncs with popular games, Razer hardware, Philips Hue, and gear from 30+ partners, supporting 16.8 million colors on individually backlit keys.

The result? Your entire desk becomes one unified light show. It's the kind of setup that makes your friends ask "wait, what keyboard is that?" right before they go order one. Affiliate gold, basically.

---

## The Extras — The Loot Table is Stacked

Beyond switches and RGB, Razer packed this thing like a collector's edition:

A multi-function digital dial with three tactile media keys gives you instant access to everything from brightness to volume in seconds, all programmable through Synapse 3. This dial is genuinely elite — no more alt-tabbing mid-game to turn down music when a teammate starts yelling.

The magnetic wrist rest snaps on at the front, made of plush leatherette for extended session comfort — and yes, it has Chroma underglow too. For marathon grind sessions or ranked ladder anxiety spirals, the wrist rest goes from "nice extra" to "I physically cannot play without this anymore."

Anti-ghosting supports up to 10 simultaneous keypresses, meaning your most chaotic multi-key combo will register cleanly. Full N-key rollover for the real sweat lords.

---

## Build Quality — Aluminum Tier, Not Plastic Peasant

The matte aluminum top frame adds real structural integrity — you're not going to flex or warp this board no matter how hard you slam it after a bad round. It feels premium in a way that $40 keyboards simply don't, and it'll survive the lifespan of several gaming PCs.

---

## Final Score: 8.5 / 10

The Huntsman Elite is a meticulously engineered piece of kit that punches above its price point in almost every category. The optical switches are legitimately faster, the RGB is unmatched for desk aesthetics, the build quality is tournament-ready, and the dial + wrist rest combo makes long sessions genuinely comfortable.

The only real knock? ABS keycaps at this price feel like getting a legendary weapon drop with a common-rarity skin. And Razer Synapse, while powerful, is software that you tolerate rather than love.

**Buy it** if you want a top-tier endgame keyboard that pulls double duty as a RGB centerpiece. **Skip it** if you're chasing pure value — something like the Keychron Q series will serve you better per dollar.
